Recycled Beverage Centre transformed into retro NES Box

Retro NES Gaming Cabinet from a Salvaged Mini-Fridge

Spotted at the curb on recycling day: a compact beverage mini-fridge, a monitor, and a pair of speakers — the raw ingredients for something much more interesting than cold drinks. After hauling them home, I stripped out the compressor, which freed up a surprisingly generous cavity perfect for housing the speakers and giving them the room they need to produce full, rich sound.

Into that reclaimed shell went a Raspberry Pi 2 running at an overclocked speed, paired with a USB NES-style controller for authentic retro feel. I configured emulators for the cartridges I own and tuned the whole stack until it ran cleanly — the result is a self-contained retro gaming cabinet that looks right at home in the living room and plays like a trip back to 1987.
